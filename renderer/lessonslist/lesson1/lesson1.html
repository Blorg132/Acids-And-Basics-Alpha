What is the Kinetic Molecular Theory?

In our world, everything that takes up space and has weight (mass/volume) is considered to be matter.
A box of cards is matter, a cup of water is matter, and YOU are even made of matter. However, matter does not appear in the same form for every case.

There are three main states of matter: liquid, solid, and gas.

Kinetic Molecular Theory (KMT):
The KMT is a scientific model that explains the behavior of gases by focusing on how their particles move. It says that gas particles are incredibly small compared to the space between them, they move in constant random motion, and they collide with each other and with container walls without losing energy. According to the KMT, there are no forces pulling gas particles together — they only interact when they collide. This theory helps scientists understand and predict how gases respond to changes in pressure, temperature, and volume, which is the foundation for gas laws you'll learn later.
